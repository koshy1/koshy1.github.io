Our Values

The principles that guide our community

Our core values guide everything we do as a community of faith. These six principles shape how we worship, serve, and live together as followers of Jesus Christ.

Healing and Belonging

"We embody a healing, joyful home"

We are committed to creating safe, trauma-informed spaces that celebrate the goodness of every member. Our community prioritizes healing from past wounds and creating genuine belonging for all people, regardless of their background or story.

Theological Rootedness

Centered on Jesus and Scripture

We are grounded in the life and teachings of Jesus Christ and the wisdom of Scripture. As part of the Presbyterian Church (USA), we honor church history, confessions, and sacraments while remaining open to contemporary spiritual movements and fresh expressions of faith.

Theological Openness

"We respond with curiosity"

We embrace theological mystery and move beyond binary thinking with an attitude of openness toward spiritual journeys. Questions are welcomed, doubt is honored, and we encourage exploration of faith that goes deeper than simple answers.

Restorative Justice

Committed to "the non-violent way of Jesus"

We pursue justice through restoration and transformation, seeking to reduce interpersonal and institutional harm. Rather than punishment, we believe in healing relationships and systems that promote equity and wholeness for all people.

Spiritual Adaptability

Open to "the wild and surprising movements of the Holy Spirit"

We remain flexible and responsive to how God is moving in our world today. This means welcoming innovation, reimagining church creatively, and being willing to change when the Spirit leads us in new directions.

Collaborative Community

Inspired by the Trinitarian concept of mutuality

We resist hierarchy and foster collaborative leadership that honors every member's voice, gifts, and imagination. Decision-making is shared, leadership is distributed, and everyone has a role in shaping our community's future.
